📊 Slide 6📖 Textbook pp. 509–511
B2 Distinguish between a vision statement, a goal and an objective. Include one example of each.
WAGOLL — What a good answer looks like

A vision statement is a broad statement of what the business aspires to become in the future. For example, IKEA's vision is "to create a better everyday life for the many people." A goal is a more specific statement of what the business intends to achieve — for example, to become the market leader in affordable flat-pack furniture in Australia within five years. An objective is the very specific, measurable action needed to achieve that goal — for example, to open three new Australian stores by June next year.

Source: Jacaranda Business Studies in Action Preliminary, Ch. 12, pp. 509–511

📊 Slides 3–4📖 Textbook pp. 499–504
C2 Explain how a small-to-medium enterprise can use a business plan to achieve its goals. Use a real business example in your response.

An Explain question requires you to make something clear by describing how or why.
WAGOLL — Paragraph structure

James Eling, owner of Marketing4Restaurants, demonstrates how a business plan can help an SME achieve its goals. Eling uses his business plan as a "road map," drawing up five-year, one-year and 90-day goals which then reveal the missing pieces in his strategy. For example, his plan's analysis of industry, customers and competitors revealed that distribution channels were his biggest hurdle — allowing him to focus effort there and improve performance. Without his plan, Eling admits his company lost focus and went "backwards" for five wasted years, illustrating that a business plan is not merely a document but an active management tool that keeps the owner accountable to their goals.

Source: Jacaranda Business Studies in Action Preliminary, Ch. 12, p. 502
