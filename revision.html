PaperTrail Stationery Co. was established in 2008 as a small sole trader business selling handmade notebooks and journals at local markets in Melbourne. Petra established HandCraft Co. two years ago, selling handmade leather goods online and through markets. She set a monthly sales target of $8,000 and a break-even point she calculated at 160 units per month (selling price $50, variable cost $20, fixed costs $4,800). In Year 1, Petra averaged 140 units per month and relied heavily on her personal savings to cover the shortfall. She had no formal business plan and made decisions based on instinct. By Year 2, she hired a business mentor who helped her create a cash flow forecast, set clearer goals, and invest in a professional website and social media advertising. Sales climbed to 200 units per month by the end of Year 2. However, a large competitor recently launched a similar product range at a lower price point, and Petra is concerned about maintaining her market share.

(a) Using the information provided, calculate HandCraft Co.'s break-even point and determine whether Petra was above or below break-even in Year 1. Show all working. (3 marks)

(b) Identify TWO critical issues that contributed to HandCraft Co.'s poor performance in Year 1 and explain how each affected the business. (4 marks)

(c) Explain how the corrective actions taken in Year 2 contributed to improved performance. Refer to specific actions from the stimulus. (4 marks)

(d) Petra is now facing a new competitive threat. Using your knowledge of critical success factors, recommend TWO strategies Petra could use to sustain her competitive advantage. Justify each recommendation. (4 marks)

(e) "A business plan is only useful when starting a business." Discuss this statement with reference to HandCraft Co. (6 marks)

Suggested split: 5 min reading, 6 min on (a), 8 min on (b), 8 min on (c), 8 min on (d), 15 min on (e), 5 min re-reading. Timer starts now.
