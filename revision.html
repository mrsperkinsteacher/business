How this week works

Monday · 15 min · Quick-fire classification
Recall and Identify
Read on screen · Write answers in your revision book

For each scenario, write in your book: (i) whether it involves a good or a service, and (ii) which role of business it best shows. Choose from: provides employment / generates profit / encourages innovation / improves quality of life / creates wealth.

1. Thanh opens a bakery and hires three local school leavers as assistants.
2. A tech startup spends two years developing an app that helps people manage their mental health.
3. A solar panel manufacturer sells panels at a price above cost and reinvests the surplus into its factory.
4. A physio clinic offers rehabilitation sessions to injured athletes.
5. A family-run farm sells fresh produce at a local market every Saturday.
6. A logistics company pays its drivers above award wages, allowing them to afford a family holiday for the first time.

Some scenarios could fit more than one role - choose the best fit and briefly say why in your book.

Tuesday · 20 min · Stimulus and classify
Application
Read on screen · Write answers in your revision book

Read the two business profiles, then write your answers to (a), (b) and (c) in full sentences in your book.

Business A - Koala Crunch Co.
Koala Crunch Co. is a snack food manufacturer based in regional Victoria. It employs 12 full-time staff and sells its products to supermarkets across New South Wales and Victoria. Last year it expanded its online store to reach customers in Queensland.

Business B - Global Freight Solutions
Global Freight Solutions is an Australian-owned logistics company with offices in Sydney, Singapore and Los Angeles. It employs over 600 staff worldwide and is listed on the Australian Securities Exchange.

(a) Classify each business by size using ABS definitions. Justify each answer. (2 marks)
(b) Classify each business by geographical spread. Justify each answer. (2 marks)
(c) Identify one role of business demonstrated by Global Freight Solutions and explain how the stimulus supports your answer. (2 marks)

Wednesday · 25 min · Apply and explain
Explain
Read the stimulus on screen · Close the laptop and write both answers in your revision book

Amara left her job as a nurse to open a mobile health screening van that visits rural communities in outback New South Wales. She invested her own savings, took on a bank loan, and now employs a part-time driver. In its first year, the business ran at a loss - but Amara says the work gives her purpose and the communities she serves now have access to health checks they previously had to drive four hours to receive.

Read the stimulus twice, then close the laptop and write your answers in your book.

(a) Explain what this scenario tells us about entrepreneurship and risk. Use evidence from the stimulus. (3 marks)

(b) Explain how Amara's business contributes to quality of life in the communities it serves. (3 marks)

Thursday · 30 min · HSC-style short answer
HSC Style
Book only - read the questions here, then close the laptop and write in your revision book as if it is an exam

Pay close attention to the command verb before you start writing.

Question 1 (3 marks) - Outline
Outline the difference between a local business and a national business. Use one example of each in your answer.

Question 2 (3 marks) - Explain
Explain how a business can contribute to wealth creation in an economy.

Outline = sketch the main features clearly and concisely. Explain = give reasons and show cause and effect. An outline is shorter and broader; an explain goes deeper into the why and how.

Weekend · 45 min · Mini case study exam practice
Exam Practice
Exam simulation - set a timer for 45 minutes

Keep this page open as your exam paper. No notes, no books, no other tabs - just this page and your revision book. Write all answers in your book. This is as close to your real exam as homework gets.

Zara worked for ten years as a pastry chef in a large hotel chain before deciding to open her own patisserie in a beachside suburb of Sydney. She invested $80,000 of her savings and took out a $40,000 small business loan. In the first year she employed two casual staff and made a small loss. By year two, her products were being stocked by three local cafes and a small specialty grocer. She now employs five full-time staff and has just launched an online delivery service, sending orders across New South Wales and the ACT. She is considering opening a second store in Melbourne.

(a) Identify the current size classification of Zara's business. Justify using ABS criteria. (2 marks)

(b) Classify Zara's business by geographical spread at TWO different points in time - when it first opened, and now. (2 marks)

(c) Outline the risks Zara took when establishing her business. (3 marks)

(d) Explain how Zara's patisserie contributes to TWO different roles of business. Use evidence from the stimulus. (6 marks)

Suggested split: 5 min reading the stimulus, 5 min on (a) and (b), 8 min on (c), 15 min on (d), 7 min re-reading and improving. Timer starts now.
